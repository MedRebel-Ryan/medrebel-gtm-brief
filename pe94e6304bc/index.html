Why Now

Launching medical SaaS in the AI era, built to win where most fail.

AI has made software cheap to build. That is exactly why most medical SaaS startups will fail, and why MedRebel is built differently.

Tools like Claude have made software cheap to build, so everyone is shipping apps fast, with no brand, no authority, and no trust. In most markets that is survivable. In medical software it is fatal. We spent 14 months building the opposite: hardened security, real compliance, an authorized integration, and a brand that signals trust before the first sales call. Medical SaaS is lucrative, but only for the companies that earn trust first. We built that foundation. The product, the security, the IP, and the brand are all in place.

What we need now

Not more building. Fuel for the marketing engine, deployed at the right moment: a major dental convention next week and another in August. The foundation is built and ripe for expansion. This is the window to turn it into growth.

The Note, In Detail

How the $50,000 note works, in plain terms.

A convertible note is a loan designed to become equity, while still protecting the lender. For family, the key point is simple: you get your money back, or you get equity worth more, whichever you prefer when the time comes. There are no monthly payments in between.

Why the terms favor you

I have not itemized the years of support or the home you gave me. Instead, that goodwill is built into your terms: a $667,000 valuation cap, lower than the $1,000,000 our early investors took, so your $50,000 converts to about 7.5% of the company instead of 5%.

No monthly installments

There are no monthly payments. The $50,000 plus 6% simple interest accrues and is settled either by converting to equity or by repayment at the next round or at maturity.

You are protected either way

At the next round you choose: take repayment of $50,000 plus accrued interest, or convert to equity (about 7.5% at the proposed cap). The low valuation cap and 20% discount make conversion strongly favorable if you want the upside.

How much equity does $50,000 buy?

If you choose to convert, your ownership depends on the valuation cap. Figures assume conversion at the cap. Your proposed cap is $667K, a discount on the $1M our early investors took.
