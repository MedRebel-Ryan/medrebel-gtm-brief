Only Authorized Open Dental Vendor in GHL

Most agencies stop at demographics. We sync the whole practice.

Nearly every GHL "integration" is a one-way push of names and numbers. Ours is a two-way clinical, scheduling, and intake bridge, live in production since Q4 2025. That is why we are authorized.

What most vendors do
  • One-way patient demographic sync: name, phone, email.
  • Breaks on invalid data, with no de-duplication.
  • No appointments, no schedule, no providers.
  • No reconciliation, so the records quietly drift apart.
What MedRebel does
  • Two-way demographics, contact info, patient status, and consent flags.
  • Full appointment sync: times, providers, operatories, procedures, and statuses (scheduled, kept, broken, no-show).
  • Patient intake and consent documents captured in GoHighLevel write back into Open Dental, so signed forms and new-patient data land in the chart with zero front-desk re-keying.
  • Provider, operatory, and status mapping between Open Dental and GHL calendars and users.
  • Schedule blockouts, historical reconciliation, and a KPI plus AI-insights analytics layer.

Built to survive production: OAuth (no stored passwords, revocable any time), the Open Dental eConnector as a secure local bridge, managed queues, and reconciliation every 15 minutes so no dropped webhook drifts the systems apart. Bad phone and email data is caught before it breaks a campaign; every failed sync is logged.
